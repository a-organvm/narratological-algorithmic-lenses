# Telos — Idealized Form

The dream and theoretical grounding.

## Vision

Narratological Algorithmic Lenses aspires to be the definitive bridge between narrative theory and computational analysis — a system where the craft wisdom of master storytellers becomes executable, testable, and generative.

## Theoretical Foundations

The project stands on five primary sources spanning 2,500 years of narrative thought:

- **Aristotle** (*Poetics*) — structure, mimesis, catharsis, the six constituent elements
- **Bharata Muni** (*Natyasastra*) — rasa theory, dosas, gunas, the holistic performance framework
- **Horace** (*Ars Poetica*) — decorum, the didactic function of art, medium awareness
- **Plato** (*Republic*) — mimetic hierarchies, the moral weight of storytelling
- **McKee** (*Story*) — contemporary screenwriting principle, controlling ideas, story value change

From these sources, 28 completed studies extract formalized algorithms from diverse practitioners: Aristotle, Bergman, Kubrick, Lynch, Tarkovsky, Tarantino, Pixar, Gaiman, Kirby, Morrison, Moore, Tolkien, Zelda, Final Fantasy, and oral traditions spanning African epic and Japanesekishōtenketsu.

## Idealized System Properties

- **Formal rigor** — Every narrative principle reduced to an algorithm with inputs, outputs, and diagnostic criteria
- **Cross-cultural reach** — Western and Eastern traditions treated with equal analytical weight
- **Multi-media applicability** — Film, comics, games, prose, oral performance analyzed through shared formal lenses
- **Diagnostic depth** — Not just description but prescription: the Script Doctor identifies what breaks and how to repair it
- **Protocol-driven** — Seven protocol levels (P1-P7) scale from triage to full craft analysis, making deep analysis accessible at any depth of engagement

## The Analyst Constellation

Nine analytical roles form the complete perspective:

| Role | Lens |
|------|------|
| Aesthete | Sensory and aesthetic dimension |
| Dramaturgist | Structural and performative architecture |
| Narratologist | Story function and narrative grammar |
| Art Historian | Visual and cultural context |
| Cinephile | Cinematic language and medium-specific craft |
| Rhetorician | Persuasion, argument, and rhetorical structure |

| Academic | Theoretical grounding and scholarly context |
