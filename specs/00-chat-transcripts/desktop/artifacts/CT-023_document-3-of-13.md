**[DRAMATURGIST]:** Movement III is the structural problem area—it disperses focus across too many parallel threads without sufficient convergence or causal connection.

| **Resolution** | [MISSING] | — | — | ABSENT |

### 2.2 Turning Point Assessment

**HOOK (Lines 1-162):** Exceptional. The quadrant sequence is formally inventive and immediately establishes the script's unique grammar. The polyrhythmic loops, the CENSORED motif, the sync-to-siren progression—this is the work of a writer with genuine visual imagination.

**INCITING INCIDENT (Line 593):** Problematic. The Black Box arrives by insect drone with no apparent causal connection to the family's actions. Questions raised but not addressed:
- Why this family?
- Why today?
- Who ordered the box?
- What triggers activation?

The inciting incident should feel inevitable in retrospect. Currently, it feels arbitrary.

**MIDPOINT (Lines 1491-1528):** Strong. Li's violent assault on Def is shocking and recontextualizing. It reveals her capacity for extreme action and suggests hidden motives. This is the moment the audience recalibrates their understanding of the protagonist.

**ALL IS LOST (Lines 2371):** Effective. Lef's death is the appropriate emotional nadir. The family has failed to protect their youngest; the future is literally destroyed. The horror of the boiling death is visceral.

**CRISIS (Lines 2717):** Underdeveloped. The moral choice game is introduced as a prose note rather than dramatized action. The mechanics are unclear:
- What exactly are the choices?
- What are the stakes for each option?
- How is the "whoever chooses first loses" rule communicated?

The script does not have a clear protagonist. Candidates:

| Character | Screen Time | Agency | Arc Completion | Verdict |
|-----------|-------------|--------|----------------|---------|
| **Li** | ~30% | HIGH (makes choices) | INCOMPLETE (complicity reveal unfinished) | BEST CANDIDATE |
| **Fre** | ~25% | LOW (reactive) | INCOMPLETE | SUPPORTING |
| **Sus** | ~20% | MODERATE | INCOMPLETE (abuse thread unresolved) | SECONDARY |
| **Rok** | ~15% | LOW (suffering) | INCOMPLETE | SECONDARY |
| **Family as Unit** | 100% | COLLECTIVE | INCOMPLETE | POSSIBLE |

**[DRAMATURGIST]:** Li emerges as the strongest protagonist candidate because:
1. She initiates the divorce (Act I action)
2. She commits unexpected violence (Midpoint revelation)
3. She is revealed as potentially complicit (Act III)

However, the script's current structure treats her as one-of-four rather than as the primary lens.

### 4.2 Recommended Protagonist Restructuring

```
CURRENT STRUCTURE:
  Li ────┐

  Sus ───┤
  Rok ───┘

RECOMMENDED STRUCTURE:
  Li (PROTAGONIST) ──────────────────────→ Primary POV
    ├── Discovery arc: Who sent the Black Box?

    └── Revelation arc: Her complicity revealed

    └── Their fates determined by Li's choices

  (Connected to spine) └─── Lef's Tank / Clock

  (Parallel film)      └─── HQ Lon Sabotage
```

**[NARRATOLOGIST]:** The fundamental structural problem is that the HQ scenes function as a separate film. They never meaningfully intersect with the family's experience until Line 2794—by which point the script terminates.

**Recommendation:** Either:
1. **ELIMINATE HQ:** Commit to *Caché*-style irresolution. Let the Black Box remain unexplained.
2. **INTEGRATE HQ:** Have HQ actions directly affect family experience. Let the family see their tormentors. Create a final confrontation.

### 6.2 Temporal Rhythm
