**Character Diagnostic**

| Dimension | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| **Want** | Freedom from marriage; unclear beyond this |
| **Need** | Unknown (arc incomplete) |
| **Flaw** | Coldness; emotional unavailability; potential cruelty |
| **Ghost** | Not established (what made her this way?) |
| **Lie Believed** | Unknown |
| **Truth to Learn** | Unknown |

**Key Scenes**
1. **Bathroom argument (412-578):** Li's tactical cruelty revealed. She absorbs Fre's insults without reaction, returns them methodically. "I don't think I wanted to" [course-correct the marriage] is her most honest line.

2. **Violence against Def (1491-1528):** The script's most recontextualizing scene. Li's capacity for extreme violence arrives without warning. The heel-to-face attack, the calm exit, the smile—this is someone who has done this before or has been waiting to.

3. **Response to Lef's death (2337):** "At least another bird is taken care of." This line is either monstrous indifference or coded acknowledgment of complicity.

4. **Rok's injury (2163-2222):** Li's "Good boy" repetition as she helps Rok suggests maternal programming—the words without the feeling. She performs care without experiencing it.

**[DRAMATURGIST] Arc Assessment:** Li is the script's most compelling character because she contains genuine mystery. The violence against Def and the coldness toward Lef's death suggest either:
- **Interpretation A:** Li is a victim of the system who has emotionally shut down
- **Interpretation B:** Li is an operative who orchestrated the Black Box activation
- **Interpretation C:** Li is discovering capacities she didn't know she had

The script's complicity reveal (line 2807) suggests Interpretation B, but it arrives too late and too quickly to land. Li needs a full recognition scene.

**Arc Completion Status:** 40% complete. Missing: discovery, choice, transformation, consequence.
