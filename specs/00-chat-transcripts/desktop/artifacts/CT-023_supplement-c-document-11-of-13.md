## 2. Dialogue by Character

### 2.1 Li's Dialogue

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Terse, tactical
- Absorbs insults without visible reaction
- Occasional dry wit
- Becomes hollow when performing maternal care

**Representative Lines:**

> "I am leaving you." (Line 211)

Direct, without softening. Li doesn't explain or justify.

> "I don't think I wanted to." (Line 566)

The most honest line in the script. When asked why she didn't try to save the marriage, Li admits she chose not to.

> "Good boy. Good boy. Good boy." (Line 2213-2217)

The repetition reveals programming without feeling. She performs comfort.

> "At least another bird is taken care of." (Line 2337)

Monstrous coldness. Either she's completely dissociated or complicit.

**Assessment:** Li's dialogue achieves appropriate opacity—we can't read her intentions, which maintains mystery. Risk: audiences may disengage from character they can't access.

### 2.2 Fre's Dialogue

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Defensive, escalating
- Self-pitying
- Uses cruelty when losing
- Occasionally reaches for poetry (always fails)

**Representative Lines:**

> "You're an unfuckable cunt. Plain and simple." (Line 508)

Fre's weapon when he's losing an argument. The escalation to this insult shows his desperation.

> "A grande gesture. We could fly to the desert... walk around without soaked shoes... could start new." (Lines 1579-1585)

His fantasy of escape with Nol—pathetic in its specificity ("soaked shoes").

> "Doesn't this mean anything?" (Line 1612)

The plea of a man who needs external validation for everything.

> "I'll save my son." (Line 2283)

His one moment of active paternal feeling, undermined by impotence.

**Assessment:** Fre's dialogue effectively conveys pathetic desperation. The bathroom argument dialogue is his strongest material. Elsewhere, he risks becoming one-note.

### 2.3 Sus's Dialogue

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Minimal
- Defensive through precision
- Uses silence as weapon
- Occasional sharp strikes

**Representative Lines:**

> "About the divorce?" (Line 690)

Delivered while drugged into euphoria—the children's complete indifference to family collapse.

> "I'm stretched fine." (Line 1207)

Resistance to Mis Sar, futile but present.

> "You'll never be as good as me." (Line 1725)

Whispered cruelty to the ballerinas—Sus has internalized competitive toxicity.

**Assessment:** Sus's dialogue is appropriately minimal for a traumatized character. More interiority needed in the moral choice scenes.

### 2.4 Rok's Dialogue

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Deflection through humor
- Cruelty as defense
- Physical focus (body, pain)
- Confusion expressed through questions

**Representative Lines:**

> "Who knows what I like? Do you know what you like?" (Line 1800)

Deflects Em's directness with philosophical uncertainty—about sexuality?

> "Kids don't solve anything, don't you know that." (Line 1805)

Rejecting the family model while trapped in it.

> "It's always raining." (Line 2064)

Statement of environmental/emotional despair.

> "I decide." (Line 2744)

His choice moment—but what does he decide?

**Assessment:** Rok's dialogue suggests depths he won't articulate. The sexuality question hovers in his refusals. More explicit internal acknowledgment needed.

### 2.5 HQ Dialogue

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Abstract
- Philosophical
- Lacking specificity
- Over-expository

**Representative Lines:**

> "Was Series B the right one?" / "It was a one." (Lines 1376-1377)

Abstract to the point of meaninglessness. What is Series B? What makes it "right"?

> "This has been one huge mistake." (Line 1664)

Jra's objection—but what specifically? The script never clarifies.

> "I guess we will see. You doubt a male's sex drive." (Line 2626)

The clearest statement of the game's gender dynamics—but it arrives very late.

**Assessment:** HQ dialogue is the script's weakest. Characters state positions without specificity. Revision should either cut entirely or add concrete stakes and character psychology.

---

### 2.6 Black Box Dialogue

**Voice Characteristics:**
- Choral (multiple female voices)
- Proclamatory
- Rhythmic repetition
- Moral authority claimed

**Representative Lines:**

> "We force families apart that should never be put back together again. You are to be splayed, weighed, and displayed. Go to your rooms." (Lines 2401-2409)

The Black Box articulates its thesis directly. This works better than HQ's abstractions because the family characters can react.

**Assessment:** Black Box voice is effective—authoritative, strange, memorable. The choral quality adds to the sense of collective judgment.

## 3. Dialogue Patterns

### Pattern 1: Call-and-Response (Li/Fre)

The bathroom argument operates through call-and-response:

```
FRE: "Every guy you fucked is an asshole."
LI: "What does that say about you?"
FRE: "What does that say about you?"
LI: "I asked first."
```

This tennis-match quality is the script's strongest dialogue mode.

### Pattern 2: Repetition (Black Box, Li)

Repetition signals programming or breakdown:

```
BLACK BOX: "Go to your rooms. Seven... Six... Five..."
LI: "Good boy. Good boy. Good boy."
```

Both suggest automated response—the house and the mother as machines.

### Pattern 3: Deflection (Children)

Sus and Rok deflect direct questions:

```
EM: "You don't like me do you?"
ROK: "Who knows what I like?"
```

The children can't answer honestly because they don't know themselves.

### Pattern 4: Over-Explanation (HQ)

HQ characters state what should be shown:

```
WOT: "You're not going to like my answer."
JRA: "Of course I won't."
```

This tells us a conflict exists but doesn't dramatize it.

## 4. Dialogue Recommendations

### Recommendation 1: Differentiate Children's Voices

Sus and Rok sometimes sound identical. Give Sus more physical awareness (she lives in her body as a dancer); give Rok more verbal aggression (he channels physicality into words).

### Recommendation 2: Cut or Transform HQ Dialogue

Current HQ dialogue adds little. Either:
- Cut entirely (let mystery stand)
- Add specific stakes ("If the ritual fails, we all die")
- Add personality (give Wot dark humor; give Jra explicit moral objection)

### Recommendation 3: Maintain Li's Opacity While Adding Tells

Li should remain hard to read, but add subtle tells that reward careful viewing. A slight pause, a repeated phrase, a glance at the camera—breadcrumbs for her complicity.

### Recommendation 4: Expand Moral Choice Dialogue

The Act III choices need full scenes with dialogue. The Black Box should articulate each choice clearly; the children should verbalize their decision-making process (even if internal monologue); consequences should be narrated or shown.
