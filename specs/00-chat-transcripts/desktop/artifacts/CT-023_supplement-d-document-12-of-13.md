## 1. Camera Taxonomy

The script establishes a comprehensive camera ecosystem. Each camera type has distinct visual and narrative functions.

### 1.1 Fixed Surveillance Cameras

**Locations:**
- Ceiling air condition duct cams
- Over-fridge cam
- Mirror light sconces
- Front door camera
- Security cameras (various)

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Wide angle
- Fixed position
- High or unusual angles (ceiling, behind objects)
- Institutional aesthetic

**Narrative Function:**
- Establishes omniscient surveillance
- Creates discomfort through unnatural angles
- Implies constant observation

---

### 1.2 Body Cameras

**Assigned to:**
- Li (construction work)
- Fre (office/domestic)
- Sus (ballet, home)
- Rok (wrestling, home)

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Movement with character
- Close-up when interacting
- Unstable during action
- POV-adjacent

**Narrative Function:**
- Creates intimacy
- Places audience with character
- Documents character's perspective

---

### 1.3 Device Cameras

**Types:**
- Mobile phones
- Tablets
- Laptops

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Vertical (phone)
- Screen-within-screen
- Selfie angle (facing camera)
- Often multiple devices visible

**Narrative Function:**
- Characters perform for devices
- Devices capture unintended moments
- Technology mediates all experience

---

### 1.4 Specialty Cameras

**Types:**
- Gutter cam (Li's arrival)
- Car interior cam
- Drone cam (Black Box delivery)
- Dream monitor (Lef)
- Television cam (watching children)

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Highly specific angles
- Often surprising perspectives
- World-building through unusual sight-lines

**Narrative Function:**
- Establishes surveillance saturation
- No space is private
- Even the gutter observes

## 2. Visual Motifs

### 2.1 CENSORED

**Description:** Pixelated/blurred areas covering genitals during intimate moments.

**Instances:**
- Fre masturbating (Line 74)
- Sus undressing (Line 101)
- Implied throughout

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Consistent blur style
- Moves with body
- Creates absence as presence

**Thematic Function:**
- Acknowledges violation while performing it
- Privacy exists only as mark of its absence
- Sexuality as only "protected" space (but still captured)

---

### 2.2 LOOP

**Description:** Footage repeating in rhythmic patterns.

**Instances:**
- Opening quadrants (Lines 17-139)
- Porn GIF (Fre watching)
- Lon reviewing footage (Line 1625)

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Frame repetition
- Polyrhythmic when multiple
- Creates hypnotic effect

**Thematic Function:**
- Characters trapped in behavioral patterns
- Life as recursive
- Time as prison

---

### 2.3 QUADRANT

**Description:** Screen divided into four sections showing different perspectives.

**Instances:**
- Opening sequence (primary)
- Potential use elsewhere (unstated)

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Four equal sections
- Different rhythms per section
- Sync moment creates unity

**Thematic Function:**
- Family as fragmented unity
- Separation within togetherness
- Surveillance capturing all simultaneously

---

### 2.4 RAIN

**Description:** Perpetual precipitation establishing world and mood.

**Instances:**
- Li's arrival (Lines 18-51)
- All exteriors
- Escalates to hail (Line 1963)

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Torrential, constant
- Blue-gray palette
- Characters always wet outdoors

**Thematic Function:**
- Environmental despair
- Isolation (keeps everyone inside)
- Grief made literal

---

### 2.5 TANK

**Description:** Floor-to-ceiling growth tank containing Lef.

**Instances:**
- Lines 841-926 (introduction)
- Lines 1936-1962 (dreams)
- Lines 2092-2395 (death)

**Visual Characteristics:**
- Vertical composition
- Liquid distortion

**Thematic Function:**

## 3. Compositional Patterns

### 3.1 Separation Within Frame

**Description:** Characters sharing space but visually isolated.

**Instances:**
- Li and Fre on opposite ends of bed (Line 201)
- Family in car, each absorbed in device (Line 928-943)
- Children watching screens, not each other

**Visual Strategy:**
- Wide shots with characters at frame edges
- Objects or space between characters
- Eye-lines that don't meet

**Thematic Resonance:**
- Physical proximity, emotional distance
- Technology as separator
- Family as collection of isolates

---

### 3.2 Direct Camera Address

**Description:** Characters looking directly into camera lens.

**Instances:**
- Sus staring through laptop (Line 364)
- Gre staring into body cam (Line 1359)
- Li reaching for camera (Line 2807)

**Visual Strategy:**
- Character's eyes meet audience's
- Breaks fourth wall
- Creates confrontation

**Thematic Resonance:**
- Awareness of being watched
- Implication of viewer
- Surveillance as relationship

---

### 3.3 Distortion/Filter

**Description:** Visual interference between camera and subject.

**Instances:**
- Fog on Rok's phone lens
- Tank glass distorting Lef
- Rain on exterior lenses

**Visual Strategy:**
- Mediation always present
- Clean sight impossible
- Technology imperfect

**Thematic Resonance:**
- We never see clearly
- Surveillance is interpretation
- Truth obscured by apparatus

---

## 4. Recommended Visual Enhancements

### Enhancement 1: Camera Failure Moments

Add instances where cameras malfunction—static, dropout, failure. This creates:
- Tension (what happens when we can't see?)
- HQ frustration (if keeping HQ plot)
- Thematic irony (surveillance isn't total)

### Enhancement 2: Establish Camera Hierarchy

Clarify which cameras are "ours" (audience POV) vs. "theirs" (HQ POV). This could be subtle—slight color grade difference, aspect ratio shift—but would clarify the surveillance architecture.

### Enhancement 3: Escalate Visual Disturbance

As the house turns hostile, camera behavior should change:
- More static/interference
- Angles become more extreme
- Cuts become more jarring
- Sound-image sync breaks

### Enhancement 4: Final Image

The script lacks a final image. Consider:
- Return to quadrant (now with death/absence)
- Single camera watching empty house
- HQ screens going dark
- Rain finally stopping
