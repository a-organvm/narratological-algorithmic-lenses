## 1. Aristotelian Correspondence

| **Mythos (Plot)** | Arrangement of incidents | INCOMPLETE | Beginning and middle present; end absent |
| **Ethos (Character)** | Moral disposition revealed through choice | WEAK | Choices introduced but not dramatized |

### 1.2 Tragic Structure Mapping

**ARISTOTLE:** "Tragedy is an imitation of an action... through pity and fear effecting the proper purgation of these emotions."

| Component | Aristotelian Requirement | Script Status |
|-----------|-------------------------|---------------|
| **Hamartia** | Flaw/error leading to downfall | PRESENT: Family dysfunction pre-exists |
| **Peripeteia** | Reversal of fortune | PRESENT: House turns hostile |
| **Anagnorisis** | Recognition | ABSENT: No character achieves recognition |
| **Catharsis** | Emotional purgation | INCOMPLETE: Horror without resolution |

**[ACADEMIC] Diagnosis:** The script achieves the setup for tragedy but fails to complete it. Lef's death generates pity and fear but lacks the recognition moment that would transform suffering into meaning.

## 2. McKee Correspondence

### 2.1 Story Values Analysis

**McKEE:** "Story is about change in a character's life values, from positive to negative or negative to positive."

| Character | Opening State | Midpoint | Closing State | Value Shift |
|-----------|---------------|----------|---------------|-------------|
| Li | Negative (trapped in marriage) | Positive? (violence as release) | UNKNOWN | INCOMPLETE |
| Fre | Negative (pathetic) | Negative (rejected) | UNKNOWN | NO SHIFT |
| Sus | Negative (abused) | Negative (isolated) | UNKNOWN | INCOMPLETE |
| Rok | Negative (repressed) | Negative (injured) | UNKNOWN | INCOMPLETE |
| Lef | Neutral (dreaming) | DEATH | — | COMPLETE |

**[NARRATOLOGIST] Diagnosis:** The script tracks negative values but doesn't complete the arc. Lef achieves the only complete value shift (existence → non-existence). Other characters are interrupted mid-arc.

### 2.2 Inciting Incident Analysis

**McKEE:** "The inciting incident radically upsets the balance of forces in the protagonist's life."

| Test | Requirement | Script Performance |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| Balance upset | Life cannot continue as before | YES: House lockdown prevents normal life |
| Protagonist response | Must respond or suffer | PARTIAL: Responses are reactive, not chosen |
| Conscious desire | Protagonist forms want | WEAK: No clear protagonist want emerges |
| Risk/stakes | Something of value at risk | YES: Lives at stake |

**Diagnosis:** The Black Box functions as inciting incident but lacks causal connection to protagonist action. It arrives from outside rather than arising from character choices.

### 2.3 Progressive Complications

**McKEE:** "Each scene should turn and raise stakes."

| Sequence | Complication | Turn? | Stakes Raised? |
|----------|--------------|-------|----------------|
| Affairs/parallel lives | Characters disperse | NO (episodic) | NO (separate threads) |
| Li's violence | Capacity revealed | YES | YES (Li recontextualized) |
| Family converges | Black Box activates | YES | YES (home becomes trap) |
| Rok's foot | First blood | YES | YES (physical stakes) |
| Lef's death | Family fails to save | YES | YES (death realized) |
| Moral choices | Game presented | YES | UNKNOWN (not dramatized) |

**Diagnosis:** Progressive complications are present but inconsistent. Movement III (parallel lives) fails to build; Movement IV recovers momentum.

## 3. South Park "But/Therefore" Correspondence

### 3.1 Causal Chain Analysis

**SOUTH PARK PRINCIPLE:** "Every scene should connect to the next through 'but' or 'therefore,' never 'and then.'"

**Sample Chain Test (Opening):**

```
Li arrives home → AND THEN
Fre masturbates → AND THEN
Sus practices → AND THEN
Rok exercises → AND THEN
All four sync → THEREFORE
Title

DIAGNOSIS: Opening operates on parallel "AND THEN" logic, justified by
formal rhythm goal. BUT: sync creates unified "THEREFORE" conclusion.
```

**Sample Chain Test (Act II):**

```
Family disperses → AND THEN
Li goes to McDonald's → AND THEN
Fre works/fucks Nol → AND THEN
Sus at ballet/Mis Sar → AND THEN
Rok wrestles/rejects Em → AND THEN
HQ observes → AND THEN
Black Box activates → ???

DIAGNOSIS: Act II operates primarily on "AND THEN" logic. The Black Box
activation has no "THEREFORE" connection to preceding events.
```

**Sample Chain Test (Act III):**

```
Black Box activates → THEREFORE
Tank heats → THEREFORE
Family converges → THEREFORE
Rok's foot crushed → THEREFORE
Family trapped → THEREFORE
Lef dies → THEREFORE
Game begins → ???

DIAGNOSIS: Act III achieves "THEREFORE" momentum. Each event causes the next.
```

### 3.2 But/Therefore Assessment

| Movement | Dominant Connector | Quality |
|----------|-------------------|---------|
| I: Overture | AND THEN → THEREFORE | ACCEPTABLE (formal justification) |
| II: Establishment | AND THEN | WEAK |
| III: Parallel Lives | AND THEN | WEAK |
| IV: Activation | THEREFORE | STRONG |
| V: Game | THEREFORE → INCOMPLETE | INCOMPLETE |

**[NARRATOLOGIST] Recommendation:** Introduce causal logic earlier. Either:
- Family actions trigger Black Box (THEREFORE)
- Black Box was ordered by Li (hidden THEREFORE)
- Algorithm selected family based on behavior (implicit THEREFORE)

## 4. Larry David "Cascading Consequences" Correspondence

### 4.1 Cascade Identification

**DAVID PRINCIPLE:** "Small initial choices create cascading consequences that intersect at climax."

| Initial Choice | Cascade | Intersection |
|----------------|---------|--------------|
| Li decides to divorce | → Files papers → Announces to Fre → Children know | PARTIAL (divorce is context, not cause) |
| Fre's porn addiction | → Caught by Nol → Affair begins → Rejection | ISOLATED (doesn't intersect) |
| Sus's perfectionism | → Mis Sar attention → Abuse → Isolation | ISOLATED |
| Rok's repression | → Gre obsession → Em rejection → Confusion | ISOLATED |

**Diagnosis:** The cascades exist but don't converge. Each character's choices create consequences within their thread but don't impact other threads.

### 4.2 Intersection Point

**DAVID PRINCIPLE:** "All cascades must intersect at a single point where consequences compound."

**Current Script:** Cascades don't intersect at Black Box activation. The Black Box arrives externally; it doesn't arise from the intersection of family choices.

**Recommended Redesign:**

```
INTERSECTION POINT: BLACK BOX ACTIVATION

Li's cascade:
├── Violence against Def → Def reports to HQ → Li flagged
                                               ↘
Fre's cascade:                                   ↘
├── Porn addiction → Data captured → Fre flagged → → HQ SELECTION
                                               ↗     ALGORITHM
Sus's cascade:                                ↗       ACTIVATES
├── Abuse suppression → Medication → Data captured
                                               ↗
Rok's cascade:
├── Sexual confusion → Search history → Data captured

RESULT: Family collectively "qualifies" for Black Box through
the intersection of their individual dysfunctions.
```

## 5. Phoebe Waller-Bridge "Three Things Going On" Correspondence

### 5.1 Layer Analysis

**WALLER-BRIDGE PRINCIPLE:** "At any moment, the character should have at least three things going on—internal want, external obstacle, and unspoken subtext."

**Test Character: Li**

| Moment | Internal Want | External Obstacle | Unspoken Subtext |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|------------------|
| Opening | Freedom | Marriage | Exhaustion |
| Bathroom argument | Divorce | Fre's resistance | Something darker |
| McDonald's | ? | ? | Meeting someone? |
| Violence vs. Def | Release | Def's presence | Practiced capacity |
| Lef's death | ? | Can't save | "Another bird" |
| Final reveal | ? | Blocked by Lon | Complicity |

**Diagnosis:** Li maintains three layers in most scenes but becomes opaque when the script needs to hide her complicity. The McDonald's scene is particularly thin—we don't know what she wants there.

### 5.2 Scene-Level Layering

**Best Example (Bathroom Argument, 412-578):**

```
LAYER 1: Surface Dialogue
├── Discussing Da's dead kids
├── Negotiating divorce logistics
└── Exchanging insults

LAYER 2: Power Dynamics
├── Li controls information flow
├── Fre escalates because he's losing
└── Temporary alliance (shared smirk)

LAYER 3: Hidden Stakes
├── Li already has exit plan?
├── Fre desperate to maintain control
└── Neither acknowledges Lef directly
```

**Diagnosis:** The bathroom scene is the script's best-layered scene. Other scenes don't achieve this density.

## 7. Attention Mechanics Correspondence

### 7.1 Gap Dynamics

**PRINCIPLE:** Narrative tension arises from the gap between expectation and outcome.

| Gap Type | Setup | Status |
|----------|-------|--------|
| **Curiosity Gap** | Who sent Black Box? | OPEN |
| **Curiosity Gap** | What is HQ? | OPEN |
| **Curiosity Gap** | Why this family? | OPEN |
| **Curiosity Gap** | What is Li hiding? | OPEN |
| **Suspense Gap** | Will Lef survive? | CLOSED (no) |
| **Suspense Gap** | Will family escape? | OPEN |
| **Suspense Gap** | What are moral choice consequences? | OPEN |

**Diagnosis:** Too many curiosity gaps remain open at script's end. The script must either close them (revelation) or commit to their openness (art-film irresolution). Current state is neither.

### 7.2 Information Distribution

**PRINCIPLE:** Strategic information distribution creates dramatic irony and suspense.

| Information | Audience Knows | Characters Know | Dramatic Effect |
|-------------|----------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Divorce planned | YES | Li, Fre | Minimal (children don't care) |
| Black Box danger | PARTIAL | NO | Suspense (countdown) |
| Li's violence capacity | YES | NO (family) | Irony/dread |
| Mis Sar abuse | YES | Sus, other ballerinas | Horror |
| Rok's desire for Gre | SUGGESTED | Unclear | Ambiguity |
| Li's complicity | REVEALED LATE | ? | Surprise (undermined by brevity) |

**Diagnosis:** Information distribution is effective for suspense but the Li complicity reveal arrives too late to function as dramatic irony. Earlier hints needed.
