# *Open View* — Comparative Analysis

**Draft:** First Draft (2018-03-29)  
**Framework:** Influence Mapping + Learning Points  

---

## 1. Primary Comparables

### 1.1 *Caché* (Hidden) — Michael Haneke, 2005

**Relevance:** Surveillance narrative, bourgeois guilt, irresolution.

**Comparison Points:**

| Element | *Caché* | *Open View* |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Surveillance source | Unknown sender of tapes | Unknown operator of Black Box |
| Explanation | Never provided | Partially provided (HQ) |
| Family dynamic | Guilt-ridden, fractured | Dysfunctional, disconnected |
| Violence | Shocking singular event | Escalating series |
| Resolution | Ambiguous | ABSENT (draft incomplete) |

**Learning Points:**
1. **Commit to irresolution OR revelation.** *Caché* never explains its mystery, which becomes the point. *Open View* introduces HQ, suggesting explanation, but doesn't deliver. Choose one approach.

2. **Surveillance can be threat without source.** The power of *Caché* is that we never know who watches. If *Open View* eliminates HQ, this tension increases.

3. **Guilt precedes surveillance.** In *Caché*, the tapes reveal guilt the protagonist already carries. The surveillance doesn't create the problem; it exposes it. *Open View* should clarify: what guilt does the Black Box expose?

---

### 1.2 *Funny Games* — Michael Haneke, 1997/2007

**Relevance:** Home invasion, sadistic games, audience implication.

**Comparison Points:**

| Element | *Funny Games* | *Open View* |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| Invaders | Two young men (present) | Black Box/HQ (absent) |
| Rules | Arbitrary, mocking genre | "Splayed, weighed, displayed" |
| Family | Affluent, loving | Affluent, disconnected |
| Violence | Sudden, off-screen, brutal | Sustained, on-screen, systemic |
| Fourth wall | Broken (remote rewind) | Suggested (camera awareness) |

**Learning Points:**
1. **Make the game rules clear.** *Funny Games*' terror comes from understanding the "game" even as it's arbitrary. *Open View*'s moral choice game needs clearer rules.

2. **Consider fourth-wall breaks.** *Funny Games* directly addresses the audience's complicity. *Open View*'s surveillance grammar implies this but could make it explicit.

3. **Cruelty with a point.** Haneke's violence critiques audience appetite for violence. What does *Open View*'s violence critique? Surveillance culture? Family dysfunction? Clarify.

---

### 1.3 *The Cabin in the Woods* — Drew Goddard, 2012

**Relevance:** Control room operators, genre deconstruction, observed victims.

**Comparison Points:**

| Element | *Cabin* | *Open View* |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Control room | Comic, humanized | Abstract, philosophical |
| Operators' stakes | World-ending if they fail | Unclear ("Series A vs B") |
| Victims' awareness | Oblivious until late | Oblivious |
| Genre | Comedic horror | Serious horror/drama |
| Resolution | Victims meet operators | Victims don't meet operators |

**Learning Points:**
1. **Humanize the watchers.** *Cabin*'s control room works because operators have personalities, jokes, concerns. HQ in *Open View* lacks this dimension.

2. **Clarify the stakes for operators.** In *Cabin*, the ritual is necessary or everyone dies. What happens if HQ fails? What are Wot, Jra, Lon risking?

3. **Consider operator-victim collision.** *Cabin*'s climax brings victims into the control room. Could *Open View* stage a confrontation between family and HQ?

---

### 1.4 *Compliance* — Craig Zobel, 2012

**Relevance:** Voice as authority, escalating degradation.

**Comparison Points:**

| Element | *Compliance* | *Open View* |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| Authority source | Phone caller | Black Box voice |
| Credibility | Claims to be police | Claims divine authority |
| Victims | Employees, young woman | Family members |
| Degradation | Sexual, psychological | Psychological, physical |
| Based on | True events | Original fiction |

**Learning Points:**
1. **Voice alone can control.** *Compliance* demonstrates how authority-by-voice compels obedience. Black Box voice should be carefully designed for maximum authority.

2. **Gradual escalation.** *Compliance* works through incremental demands. *Open View* jumps to extremity (Lef's death) quickly. Consider more gradual escalation.

3. **Complicity of victims.** *Compliance*'s horror is that victims participate in their degradation. The moral choice game could emphasize family members complicit in hurting each other.

---

### 1.5 *Unfriended* — Leo Gabriadze, 2014

**Relevance:** Screen-native grammar, surveillance aesthetic.

**Comparison Points:**

| Element | *Unfriended* | *Open View* |
|---------|--------------|-------------|
| Visual format | Single laptop screen | Multi-cam surveillance |
| Teen characters | Yes (victims) | Yes (Sus, Rok) |
| Secrets revealed | Cyberbullying guilt | Family dysfunction |
| Supernatural | Ghost in the machine | Unknown (HQ? AI?) |
| Budget | Ultra-low | Mid-budget |

**Learning Points:**
1. **Format as constraint = innovation.** *Unfriended*'s laptop-screen format creates its identity. *Open View*'s surveillance grammar should be equally committed.

2. **Secrets drive horror.** *Unfriended* kills characters when their secrets are exposed. Could *Open View* more explicitly connect deaths/suffering to exposed secrets?

3. **Technical execution matters.** *Unfriended* succeeds through seamless technical execution of its conceit. *Open View*'s multi-cam approach requires equal commitment.

## 3. Differentiation Assessment

**What *Open View* offers that comparables don't:**

1. **Visual Grammar:** The quadrant opening and surveillance-as-form approach is formally innovative—neither *Caché* nor *Unfriended* achieves this specific aesthetic.

2. **Family Interiority:** Unlike *Cabin* (victims are types) or *Compliance* (workplace), *Open View* attempts complex family psychology.

3. **Child Stakes:** Lef's death and children's trauma go further than most comparables in using family vulnerability.

4. **Thematic Density:** The layered themes (surveillance, futurity, family, complicity) are more ambitious than pure genre exercises.

**Where *Open View* currently lags comparables:**

1. **Resolution:** All comparables achieve their endings; *Open View* doesn't.

2. **Clarity:** *Cabin* and *Compliance* are crystal-clear in their mechanics; *Open View* is murky.

3. **Operator characterization:** *Cabin*'s control room operators are vivid; HQ is abstract.
