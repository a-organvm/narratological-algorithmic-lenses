## 3. Marketing Strategy

### Positioning Statement

"From the fractured screens of a surveillance society, a family's darkest secrets become weapons in a deadly game."

### Key Selling Points

1. **Formal Innovation:** Multi-camera surveillance aesthetic positions as "next evolution" of screen horror
2. **Elevated Horror Credibility:** Haneke comparisons for critical positioning
3. **Timely Themes:** Surveillance culture anxiety resonates broadly
4. **Cast Potential:** Four lead roles offer diverse casting opportunities

### Marketing Challenges

1. **Violence Against Children:** Lef's death is the hardest sell; marketing must navigate without exploiting
2. **Abuse Subplot:** Sus/Mis Sar thread requires careful positioning
3. **Incomplete Narrative:** Current draft cannot be marketed (must complete)
4. **Tonal Ambiguity:** Is it art-film or genre? Marketing must commit

### Trailer Strategy

```
STRUCTURE:
├── Open with quadrant imagery (visual hook)
├── Establish family dysfunction (relatable entry)
├── Introduce Black Box (mystery/threat)
├── Escalate to house lockdown (genre momentum)
├── Avoid Lef's death (save for experience)
└── End with "Go to your rooms" voice (memorable tag)

TONE: Dread-building, not shock-based
MUSIC: Original score or licensed dissonance
AVOID: Explicit violence; children in danger imagery
```

## 4. Distribution Recommendations

### Optimal Path

```
1. FESTIVAL PREMIERE (Sundance/Toronto)
   └── Acquisition by A24, Neon, or Focus Features

2. LIMITED THEATRICAL
   └── Platform release in major markets
   └── Expand based on performance

3. VOD/STREAMING
   └── Premium VOD 3 weeks post-theatrical
   └── Streaming window 6-9 months

4. INTERNATIONAL
   └── Territory-by-territory sales
   └── French and German art-house distributors
```

### Alternative Paths

**Path B: Direct-to-Streaming**
- Partner with Netflix, Hulu, or Shudder
- Skip theatrical, maximize reach
- Risk: diminished prestige

**Path C: International First**
- Premiere at Venice or Berlin
- Build European credibility
- Return to US for acquisition
- Risk: US market cooling

## 5. Audience Research Questions

For test screenings, assess:

1. **Comprehension:** Do audiences understand the surveillance conceit?
2. **Engagement:** Where does attention wane? (Likely: HQ scenes)
3. **Emotional Response:** What do audiences feel during Lef's death?
4. **Resolution Satisfaction:** Does the ending satisfy genre expectations?
5. **Repeat Viewership:** Would audiences watch again knowing the "game"?
