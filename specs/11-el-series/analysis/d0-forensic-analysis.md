# D0 FORENSIC ANALYSIS
## "A Girl and Her Dogs" (2017-10-31 Baseline Draft)

---

## I. STRUCTURAL OVERVIEW

**Total Page Estimate:** ~55-60 pages (based on line count and formatting density)

**Act Structure Proportions:**
- **Act I (Setup):** Scenes 1-12 | Family dynamics, job search tension, dog-walking business establishment
- **Act II-A (Complication):** Scenes 13-22 | El-Sheen relationship development, intimacy, routine
- **Act II-B (Crisis):** Scenes 23-35 | Sheen's death discovery, investigation initiation, evidence gathering
- **Act III (Resolution):** Scenes 36-42 | Confrontation attempts, family intervention, Wynwood decision

**Structural Signature:** The draft employs a **delayed inciting incident** architecture—Sheen's death occurs well past the midpoint, inverting conventional thriller structure. The first half functions as character study; the second half as procedural.

**Stage Direction Style (Act I):**
- Heavy use of parenthetical behavioral notes: "(staring at her phone)", "(not looking up)"
- Verb choices favor **continuous action**: "pouring," "scrolling," "ignoring"
- Minimal camera language—prose-first approach

**Character Voice Patterns:**

**SANDRA:** Interrogative mode dominant. Questions stack without waiting for answers:
> "What are you going to do? Did you call back? Have you thought about—"

**DAVE:** Deflection through logistics. Responds to emotional content with practical data:
> "Traffic's going to be bad. I should leave."

**HUGH:** Associative rambling. Monologues spiral through tangents:
- Topic A → personal anecdote → unrelated observation → return to Topic A variant
- Speeches run 8-15 lines without interruption
- Other characters' responses reduced to minimal acknowledgment

**EL:** Zero dialogue. Communication through:
- Pre-written cards (physically handed to characters)
- Notepad writing (characters read aloud)
- Text messages (shown on screen or read by recipients)

**Mac and Cheese Scene — Micro-Analysis:**

This extended sequence (spanning approximately 3 pages) functions as the draft's **emotional center**. Craft elements:

1. **Real-time duration:** No time cuts. Cooking action anchors continuous present.
2. **Dialogue sparsity:** Sheen carries 90% of spoken content; El's responses are physical
3. **Stage direction density:** Every beat of cooking process is choreographed
4. **Intimacy mechanics:** Physical proximity escalates through shared task

**Investigation Mechanics:**

El's mute condition creates unique procedural constraints:
- **Polaroid documentation:** El photographs evidence, creating physical archive
- **Text-based interviews:** Contacts communicated via SMS
- **Digital research:** Tumblr, Instagram used as investigative tools
- **Physical presence:** Surveillance without verbal confrontation

**Stage Direction Shift (Act II-B):**
- Verb choices become **active/aggressive**: "stalks," "examines," "follows"
- Camera language emerges: "WE SEE," "CLOSE ON," "POV"
- Scene transitions accelerate—montage grammar increases

### ACT III: CONFRONTATION AND RESOLUTION

| Scene | Location | Function | Rhythm |
|-------|----------|----------|--------|
| 25 | INT. FAMILY HOME - LIVING ROOM | Family intervention redux | **Structural echo** — mirrors Scene 2 |
| 26 | INT. EL'S ROOM | El receives Wynwood email | **External validation** — art world recognition |
| 27 | INT. FAMILY HOME - LIVING ROOM | El reads email aloud | **Voice emergence** — first vocalization |

**Ending Analysis:**

The draft's conclusion hinges on El's **first vocalized words**: reading the Wynwood gallery acceptance email aloud to her family. Craft implications:

1. **Voice as choice:** El can speak; she chooses when/whether to
2. **Content determines form:** Art recognition unlocks verbal expression
3. **Audience shift:** Speaking for herself, not in response to others' demands

**Final Image:** El declines the Wynwood offer—asserting agency through rejection.

## V. STAGE DIRECTION STYLE ANALYSIS

### Verb Patterns by Act

**Act I:** Continuous present, domestic verbs
- "pouring," "scrolling," "chewing," "ignoring," "watching"

**Act II-A:** Intimate, gradual verbs
- "reaching," "touching," "waiting," "observing," "settling"

**Act II-B:** Active, investigative verbs
- "stalks," "photographs," "examines," "follows," "documents"

**Act III:** Declarative, decisive verbs
- "stands," "faces," "reads," "speaks"

### Camera Language Emergence

Minimal in Acts I-II-A. Increases significantly in investigation sequences:
- "WE SEE" appears 4 times in Act II-B
- "CLOSE ON" used for evidence details
- "POV" employed sparingly for El's surveillance perspective

### Parenthetical Usage

Heavy reliance on behavioral parentheticals throughout:
- "(without looking up)"
- "(barely audible)"
- "(to herself, but we hear)"

This suggests a writer thinking in **performance terms** rather than pure page economy.

## VI. STRUCTURAL ANOMALIES

### Unconventional Elements

1. **Delayed inciting incident:** Sheen's death occurs at approximately 55% mark
2. **Protagonist silence:** Zero traditional dialogue from lead character
3. **Inverted thriller structure:** Character study precedes procedural
4. **Dog-to-human subtitles:** Animals' "dialogue" translated for audience (limited use)
5. **Digital investigation:** Tumblr/Instagram as narrative tools (2017-era social media)

### Pacing Observations

- **Act I:** Deliberate, domestic rhythm. Risk: feels slow without clear dramatic question.
- **Act II-A:** Relationship development without external conflict. Risk: audience patience.
- **Act II-B:** Accelerated procedural. Risk: tonal whiplash from earlier acts.
- **Act III:** Compressed resolution. Risk: insufficient payoff for investigation threads.

---

## VII. THEMATIC INFRASTRUCTURE

### Central Tensions

1. **Voice vs. silence:** El's muteness as choice, not limitation
2. **Family surveillance vs. autonomy:** Domestic panopticon El navigates
3. **Institutional failure:** Police inability/unwillingness to investigate
4. **Art as communication:** Visual/written expression over verbal
5. **Female vulnerability:** Sheen's death within pattern of gendered violence
