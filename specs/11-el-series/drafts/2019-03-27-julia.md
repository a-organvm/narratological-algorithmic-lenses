INT. MANSION FOYER - NIGHT

A bright blinding light turns on above--

A massive and eccentric glass chandelier over head illuminates a pre-industrial royal chateau. The dome above is painted in Christian idols.

JULIA (20s) appears at the top of the grand staircase in long and spilling robes. She descends, a halo hovers around her head..

The halo around her fades to a crown of thorns.

The deafening disorienting music fades out.

El, on her hands and knees, backs away from Julia.

Julia cups El’s cheeks and El involuntarily stands.

JULIA
Look at you.

They lock eyes in a long silence. Julia’s smile widens, and El cowers.

JULIA
Let’s eat.

INT. JULIA’S DINING ROOM

Two empty seats in the middle of a long wooden ornate table dressed with empty expensive silverware. Ten Barely-Clad woman nervously sit on their hands to each side of the empty chairs.

Julia pulls the true middle seat out for El to sit. And takes the other empty chair next to her.

As Julia sits, the room comes to life with dull chatter.

JULIA
We do this night to night. Free to say what we want, dress how we want. Pretty simple.

Julia stirs a wooden spoon in the water of a metallic pitcher, then pours the fluid into a wine-glass before El.

El reaches for the wine-glass, and holds it in her lap, grasping the stem with both hands, she nervously twirls it.

JULIA
(guides the glass to El’s lips)
Drink.

El takes a small sip, and gagging on the water she.pulls the glass away from her mouth.

JULIA
Driiiink.
(pushes the glass into El’s mouth, and forces her to down the entire glass with a manicured fingertip)
A little bitter.

El recoils from the foul-tasting water.

Julia finishes all the fluid from her own glass in a delicate sip.

JULIA
(licks her lips)
But you get used to it.

El sits tightly-wound in her chair, grasping the rim of the seat with her hands.

JULIA
We don’t normally get to dinner. No one gets hungry.

Julia grabs El’s plate, and flings it across the room.

She laughs for three tight seconds. Then slaps the thigh of the girl next to her. 

The girl gets up, removes the robes from Julia’s shoulders, and 

massages her back.

JULIA
It’s impressive, no?...All of this, I’ve made it. My brothers are here and taken care of. A successful importing and exporting business. It feels so good to be able to accomplish tenfold what when at one-fold was wildly thought of as out of my capabilities.

Julia refills their wine glasses. 

JULIA
Tell me--did you ever get your head stuck in between the railings ?...little Chad ever try and play doctor with you?

Julia sips from her glass, no longer concerned with whether or not El drinks hers. 

JULIA
Is it true the children in this country get fifty presents on Christmas?

El can’t help but feel wonderful. Her pupils dilate, the lids close. All the tension releases from her body. 

Julia wraps El’s fingers around the wineglass. El takes small sips. 

JULIA
My father said to me, when I was smaller than you, before my momma stopped giving him children--he said: I’m dying...I’m dying and I wanted a boy...every father does. But tis not what God allowed. 

JUDA whispers into Julia’s ear. 

JUDA
Tell us her name!

PETRA materializes from behind El’s chair, hissing. She runs her fingertips up and down El’s forearm. 

PETRA
Aye!

CHORUS
Tell us her name! Tell us her name!

JULIA
It’s her name to give. 

The rest of the girls surround El, and caresses her arms and legs. They run their hands through her hair. 

JULIA
She doesn’t need a name here. 

El laughs with no regard. 

The laugh slides into a sloppy giggle. 

El loses control of her eyelids, they keep falling, and as they do the whole screen falls black. 

El slithers amongst writhing vines, she doesn’t fight the tendrils enveloping her entire body. 

She is experiencing bliss for the first time; a careless bliss. 

JULIA
In most ways, we’re glad she’s dead.

Somewhere far off an orchestra of cries, shouts, groans, and moans grows louder. 

JULIA
She helped you stand out...She brought you to us.

Light from a screen dances across El’s face. She forces her eyes open.

With all of her might she tries to push away from what she sees, but the apostles hold her still.

JULIA
However, you’ve stuck your little nose in places it did not belong.

The sound from the films cut out. The apostles disappear.

El faces a bay of screens from floor to ceiling. Each screen is tuned into a different camera angle from El’s apartment complex.

JULIA
What were those dates you said you were curious about again?...Yes, of course, you did not say anything.

All screens tune to one feed: El's front door: Sandra asleep on the couch: Dave sitting in the dark kitchen, staring at the wall: El’s bedroom, Albie asleep on her bed.


**JULIA’S BEDROOM (ON THE SCREENS)**

The Apostles, singing, light an ungodly amount of candles around a red-velvet bed with wrought-iron bed-posts. 

JULIA
Tell me, how much do you think you’re worth?

El pushes herself out of her seat--

Large, leather-gloved hands grab her shoulders. Alek locks her in a bear-hug. He covers her mouth with one hand. 

El submits.

JULIA
We will find out.


.LONG WINDING STAIRCASE

Alek carries El up the spiral stone staircase. El, her body limp, and her eyes searching for a way out--

Alek huffs, a bit out of breath. 

His foot misses a step, his shin slams into stair nosing, and he thrusts one arm out to catch them from falling.

One leathered-gloved hand remains covering El’s jaw--

She opens her chomps and bites into Alek’s hand with all of might.

Alek winces in pain, and they slide down the stairs a few steps  before he catches them again--

As he frees his hand from the glove, his flesh RIPS.

Blood pours down his hand and forearm.

El spins around, on higher-ground,

She lifts a knee to her chest and kicks him.

In Alek’s compromised position, he falls onto his back and tumbles down the stairs and out of sight. 

El takes the stairs down two-at-a-time.

At the bottom of the staircase,

Awaits no Alek.
